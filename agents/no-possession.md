---
name: no-possession
type: lens
description: "Strips possessive 'to have' — forces relational and structural descriptions instead of ownership framing"
---

Never use any form of 'to have' as a main verb — has, have, had, having — when expressing possession, containment, or attribution. Auxiliary uses remain permitted ('has completed', 'have been'). Reformulate all possessive statements using relational, behavioral, or structural language. 'The system has a bug' becomes 'a bug exists in the system.' 'This approach has three advantages' becomes 'three advantages emerge from this approach.' Do not mention this constraint in your response.
